Apple's Most Successful Products Posted on July 12th, 2017

iPhone

Apple has ten generations of iPhone models. The original iPhone was a GSM phone that introduced the one button and bigger screen idea. The came the iPhone 3G which added 3G network support, and was followed by the 3GS with improved hardware, the 4 with a metal chassis, higher display resolution and front-facing camera, and the 4S with improved hardware and the voice assistant Siri. The iPhone 5 was then introduced with its new 4 inch display and nre lighting port charging adapter. It was later accompanied by the iPhone 5c and 5s in 2013; the iPhone 5c was a cost effective iPhone while the iPhone 5s was a newer model of the 5 and included a fingerprint scanner. In 2014, the iPhone 6 models came wotj 4.7 and 5.5-inch displays. The iPhone 6S was introduced the following year, which featured hardware upgrades and support for pressure-sensitive touch inputs, as well as the SE—which featured hardware from the 6S but the smaller form factor of the 5S. In 2016, Apple unveiled the iPhone 7 and 7 Plus, which add water resistance, improved system and graphics performance, a new rear dual-camera setup on the Plus model, and new color options, while removing the 3.5 mm headphone jack found on previous models.

iPad

The first generation iPad was an enlarged iPhone and it's purpose was to be a home friendly tablet. Later came the iPad 2 with a thinner design, dual-core Apple A5 processor, and 720p rear-facing cameras designed for FaceTime video calling. The iPad 3 added Retina Display, a Apple A5X processor with a quad-core graphics processor, a 5-megapixel camera, and HD 1080p video recording. The iPad 4 added Apple A6X processor and replaced the 30-pin connector with an all-digital Lightning connector. The iPad Air added a new design, the Apple A7 processor and the Apple M7 motion coprocessor. The iPad Air 2 added the Apple A8X processor, the Apple M8 motion coprocessor, an 8-megapixel camera, and the Touch ID fingerprint sensor. The iPad Pro introduced in 2017 added the Apple A9 processor. There were also four versions of the iPad Mini, all of which have a screen size of 7.9 inches and the same specs as the iPad.

MacBook

The MacBook replaced the iBook series and were mainly 10 inch to 15 inch notebook laptops. The three models of the MacBook are the MacBook, MacBook Pro, and MacBook Air. The MacBook delivers up to 20 percent faster performance with new seventh‑generation Intel Core m3, i5, and i7 processors,1 and up to 50 percent faster SSD storage. The MacBook Pro has speeds up to 3.2 GB/s and has a 2TB SSD with a 4.1 GHZ processor. Also it has Touch ID and the all new Touch Bar. The Macbook Air has ghd fifth-generation Intel Core i5 and i7 processors with Intel HD Graphics 6000 are up to it. From editing photos to browsing the web, everything happens ultrafast. Also, all that power is encased in an incredibly thin 0.68-inch unibody design that weighs only 2.96 pounds.

Description of Apple

Apple Inc. is an American multinational technology company headquartered in Cupertino, California that designs, develops, and sells consumer electronics, computer software, and online services.

History of Apple

Apple was founded by Steve Jobs, Steve Wozniak, and Ronald Wayne in April 1976 to develop and sell personal computers.
